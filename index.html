Hello everyone, we are group 2, I’m Ayden. I’m Yujin. I’m Laura. In this video, we are going to discuss the topic of architectural theory, such as its definition, how it is represented, and why it is significant through our research analysis and diagrams of Charles Gwanthey House, Kyoto Villa garden, and Toyo Ito Constellation builds. Diagram Analyzation: First, our house diagram is about the “Gwathmey Residence”, one of Charles Gwathmey’s most famous works with him using nontraditional design approaches and geometric forms, his building functions both as a house and a sculptural object within the landscape. For the analytic drawing, I focused on how the form of the structure creates atmosphere and separates different programs. Inside the building, the design prominently features geometric shapes. For example you can see at the parts highlighted, cubes appear in windows and thresholds, while the sunlight creates additional reflective patterns across the interior. Moreover, there’s a large window by the door, further emphasizing the forms and making the space feel visually extensive.

Diagram Analyzation: I was mainly fascinated by the unique impression that was left upon those who experienced the Kyoto Villas. With the classic, traditional interior of the Kyoto Villas, the exterior views and historic structures were marked to create a “themed experience” for individuals. This, to me, was one of the greatest examples of how “architecture is not only complete by form, but also through intricate details like scent, smell, touch, and emotions.” Orientations Reading “The Kissing Architecture”: The term kiss is used by Sylvia Lavin to define contemporary architectural performance, mainly ones with ephemeral (short-lived) qualities.

The reading explores how temporal beauty is often avoided in architectural practices as the community longs for ‘masterful, permanent’ structures – through references to multiple artists, the writer shows how powerful temporal architecture can transform and evoke intimate emotions that cannot be imitated without the fluidity kissing architecture can offer.
